What is a Project Manager?

  • Definition: The individual fully responsible for planning, leading, executing, and closing projects.
  • Responsibilities: Managing teams, budgets, timelines, and risks to achieve goals.
  • Critical Role: Acting as the "bridge" between strategic business vision and technical results.

Without an effective PM, projects are vulnerable to scope creep and cost overruns.

SDLC Basic Concepts

Definition & Relationship

Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a systematic framework for creating high-quality software.


In the context of IT Project Management, SDLC is the technical methodology executed during the Execution phase.

Common SDLC Phases

  • Requirement Analysis
  • System Design
  • Development (Coding)
  • Testing (QA)
  • Deployment
  • Maintenance

Key Project Roles

Project Manager

Strategic leader. Focuses on "Managing Process". Responsible for overall project success.

Project Team

Technical experts (Developers, Designers, Analysts). Focuses on "Managing Work". Responsible for producing the product.

Stakeholders

Clients, Sponsors, or Users. Focuses on "Requirement & Value". Provides input and approval.

Project Planning Skills

Failing to plan is planning to fail. 5 vital planning elements:

  • Scope: Defining what is In-scope and Out-scope.
  • Timeline: Creating realistic schedules and milestones.
  • Budget: Estimating resource and tool costs.
  • Risk: Identifying potential issues and mitigation plans.
  • Resource: Allocating the right human resources for each task.

"A goal without a plan is just a wish."

The Project Management Triangle

The Triple Constraint

Project quality is constrained by three interdependent elements:


  • Time: How fast?
  • Cost: How cheap?
  • Scope: How many features?

Golden Rule: You cannot change one side without affecting the others.

Project Execution

Team Coordination

Execution is the phase where plans become reality. The key is communication routines:

  • Daily Standup: Quick 15-minute update.
  • Weekly Sync: Weekly progress review.

Managing Issues & Deliverables

Problems will arise. A PM's job isn't to avoid them, but to manage them (Issue Management).

The team's focus must remain on Deliverables that meet quality specifications.

Leadership & Team Management

More Than Just a Boss

A Project Manager is a servant leader. Key duties:

  • Building Trust Creating psychological safety.
  • Conflict Management Mediating differences into constructive solutions.
  • Motivation Giving appreciation and reminding the team of the big picture.

Monitoring & Control

To keep the project On Track, a Project Manager must be able to answer 3 key questions at any time:

1
Status

Where are we now?

2
Achievement

What is already done?

3
Remaining

What still needs to be done?

Tools: Progress Report, Burndown Chart, Gantt Chart Tracking.

Communication Skills

"90% of a Project Manager's job is communication."

To the Team

Use clear language, precise technical details, and unambiguous instructions.

To Stakeholders

Avoid technical jargon. Focus on business impact, budget, timeline, and risks.

Reporting

Reports must be honest, concise, and dense. Deliver bad news as soon as possible.

Closing & Go Live

The Finish Line

Crucial final phase to ensure project value is delivered:

  • Go Live: Official launch to users.
  • Handover: Transfer of documents, access, and operations.
  • Lessons Learned: Documenting successes and failures for future learning.
  • Celebration: Celebrate the team's hard work!
